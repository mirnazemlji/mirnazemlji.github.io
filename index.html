Mirna Džamonja, logician

I am a researcher working in logic and its manifestations in mathematics, computer science and philosophy. The foundations of my work are in ZFC set theory, including large cardinals, and its connections with any of the subjects mentioned above.

logique.consult at gmail dot com

In addition to my mathematical work, I take seriously the idea of an engaged intellectual and believe that the academic community has a duty of having an opinion.

Ethics in Research

As scientists, we are charged with the idea of truth and the duty to never spoil it. As human beings, dealing with other human beings such as students and collegues, we have a responsability of treating them rightly. Here are some items of ethics that I believe should be respected in the profession of a research mathematician and university professor. This list is not exclusive neither does it apply only to our profession.

  • Relation with Colleagues

May sound obvious, but never discriminate. Under any circumstances, even if you think the person in front of you is completely worthless in some other aspects of their life, deal with the person on the professional basis and do it the same way you would do with everybody else. This includes direct and indirect contact, mathematical conversations as well as anonymous referee reports and hiring decisions.

  • Relation with Students

Respect, encouragement and fairness. You are there to educate a new generation. They trust you, the society trusts you. Never do anything to make you untrustworthy. Mark fairly, do your best to teach what you know, accepting critics if necessary. As a Ph.D. supervisor, give your best and do not count credits. I have kept the advice given to me by people I respect in the UK: the Ph.D. thesis is the student's work. Do not boost your publication list by publishing somebody's thesis, while of course, if there is material for a joint paper where you have actually contributed, you may propose that to the student.

  • Giving credits

Give all the credit due: to co-workers, to people who inspired you with an idea and to AI Assistants if you used them. No exceptions. Do not try to change the history of ideas if you happen to improve somebody's else idea, even significantly. Just tell it as it is.

  • Using other people's work

Use only the work that is meant to be in the public domain. This does not include research proposals, drafts that people sent you before they had a chance to publish, ideas that they unwittingly told you thinking they were speaking to a friend, or the projects on which somebody was working for a long time and you have a great method to solve it just after they present it to you. Rather, tell them that you think such a method could be helpful to them in solving the problem and if needed, help them apply it and be happy if they thank you in the acknowledgments, as they should.

  • Proof standards

Do not claim that something is a proof if you know that it is not. An approximation is not a proof, it is an idea. Do not play on the fact that you are so good that you are not likely to make a mistake. Read your own proofs. Everybody can make a mistake: if you do, make sure that you make the knowledge of the mistake pulic as soon as you can.

  • Respect mathematcs

Never, ever, use mathematics to communicate anything else but mathematics. No hidden messages or meanings, no spying, no insinuations. Do not attempt to lower the field to the level where you happen to be if you are thinking of applying these methods.

A bit of a Chat

I studied in Yugoslavia and USA, with a Ph.D from UW-Madison, with Ken Kunen. Went as a postdoc at the Hebrew University in Jerusalem with Menachem Magidor and Saharon Shelah, to continue as an Assistant Professor at UW-Madison. Held various grades at the University of East Anglia, UK, up to the Professor of Pure Mathematics (named 2010). Since 2020 I am in Paris, where after being a CNRS researcher for a while, I was an Associated Researcher at IHPST (Université Panthéon-Sorbonne) and IRIF (Université Paris Cité) until 2024. Have had many visiting positions around the world and was a domestic member of the BiH Academy ANUBiH, also until 2024.

I hold titoist political views and am against any political blocks and any wars. A great believer in diplomacy and mediation in politics and justice and law in an organised social system.

For a long time our political discourse was dominated by the division between the left and the right. I believe that this division no longer makes sense. I support the politics that joins people around a common sense agenda. In BiH I have proudly supported SDP , which happens to be a leftwing party promoting BiH as a secular country not divided by ethnic lines. In France, I am one of the early day supporters of our President Macron, who through his idea of En Marche ! opened my eyes to the fact that division along left and right belongs firmly in the past (in France, it belongs to 1789!).

In the United States of America, where I lived for almost ten years and I closely follow the politics, I strongly support President Trump. A man who knows and loves his country and can talk to everybody, knows how to consult and listen and knows how to think quickly and logically. I am a great supporter of the European Union and although we still have things to work on, don't forget that we are so young ! One of my favourite leaders in Europe is Giorgia Meloni in Italy, I think that she is not only somebody who changes Italy for the best every day, but is also one of the most human and correct voices on the question of illegal immigration. A problem that needs to be solved by stopping the gangs of passers exploiting vulnerable poeple by bringing them to the promised land in which they in fact become slaves of these gangs to repay the price of the passage.

In addition to BiH, I am a citizen of Belgium and of the UK. The Belgian model is a miracle of democracy and dipolomacy. I hope it can one day be applied to BiH. We have a king in Belgium and it is this nonpartisan and trusted presence that makes the Belgium model work. Whenever the Wallons and the Flamands come to what seems an unsolvable problem, the King steps in and negotiates. Sometimes for many months. Finally the problem gets solved and we carry on. We don't have a king in BiH, but I hope that the EU can act in this way for us, leading us slowly as we discover ways to put the three angry brothers representing our main nationalities back to the common family that we form.

The UK, another Kingdom where I belong. I am still learning the democracy in the UK, as it is very complex and needs to be viewed by taking into account the history, which is not the same as the continental history which I know better. Plus, the Brits are discrete people who don't really talk about themseleves enough, personally or publicly. But I think I figured out a thing or two. One is that I am (discretly, of course:-) really in love with Britain. They've got style ! I strongly wish that Britain rejoins the EU. In the country, I personally wish that the King gets more political power, because sometimes one can feel the vacuum left by warrying politicians and one would wish that the reason steps in. This is not against democracy, just the opposite.

And where would a good titoist be without a strong interest in international politics. I think that the greatest, visionary, position that Tito and others in the non-alligned movement held is that every country needs to develop in its own way, knowing the best its own specificities. I naturally feel a connection with India, the heart of this movement from the times of Naser, Naser, Tito and Sukarno, to mention the most prominent ones. For me, that vision of international politics is represented today by the Indian Prime Minister Narenda Modi. I follow and admire what he is doing in India, that huge country-continent with an amazing history and, I believe, even a more amazing future.
