A Bit of a Chat

I studied in Yugoslavia and the USA. I obtained my Ph.D. from the University of Wisconsin–Madison with Ken Kunen. I then went as a postdoc to the Hebrew University of Jerusalem, working with Menachem Magidor and Saharon Shelah. I continued as Assistant Professor at UW–Madison, and later held positions at the University of East Anglia (UK), where I became Professor of Pure Mathematics in 2010.

Since 2020 I have been based in Paris. I worked as a CNRS researcher and then as an Associated Researcher at IHPST (Université Panthéon-Sorbonne) and IRIF (Université Paris Cité) until 2024.

I also worked at the Mathematical Institute of the Czech Academy of Sciences in Prague.

I have had many visiting positions worldwide and was a member of the Academy of Sciences and Arts of Bosnia and Herzegovina until 2024.

I hold Titoist political views and am strongly against political blocks and wars. I believe in diplomacy, mediation, and the rule of law in organised societies.

For a long time, political discourse was dominated by the left–right divide. I believe this division no longer makes sense. I support politics that brings people together around common sense solutions and pragmatic approaches.

I am a strong supporter of the European Union, while believing that major reforms are necessary. In particular, I favour a Europe of sovereign nations cooperating through cultural and economic links rather than through excessive centralised bureaucracy. Mildly speaking, I do not like the fact that the European Union marks strong borders with ``my land". These are the Slavic countries, obviously including Russia, and all the Balkan countries.

I am a citizen of Bosnia and Herzegovina, Belgium, and the United Kingdom. I greatly admire the Belgian model of democracy and I hope that similar principles of dialogue and mutual respect can help my home country of Bosnia and Herzegovina.

I have deep affection for Britain and continue to learn about its unique democratic traditions. I hope Britain will one day rejoin a reformed European Union.

As a Titoist, I remain inspired by the original spirit of the Non-Aligned Movement — the idea that every country should develop according to its own specificities and needs, while maintaining respectful international relations.
